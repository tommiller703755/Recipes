Lemon Veggie Pasta

The first Christmas I spent with my girlfriend's family was an unusual experience to ssay the least, involving light up pajama pants, chocolate cake shots, a kickball, and a Christmas lasagna. Her family isn't italian, but you wouldn't be able to tell that from how they cook.

As I helped prepate this lasagna, I learned her family's tips and tricks for this recipe. Of these, the most critical were to sprinkle herbs on top, always use Italian sausage, and to prepare the lasagna the day before you bake it.

This last tip is the true beauty of the recipe. If the lasagna is left to sit in the fridge overnight, the flavors of eack layer will meld together beautifully. This also works in a freezer, so you can make this well before you'll need it and keep is stashed away.

Ingredients:

  • ½ a pound package of lasagna
  • 1½ pounds of italian sausage
  • 1 cup of chopped onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) of crushed tomatoes, undrained
  • 2 cans (6 ounces each) of tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons of sugar
  • 2½ teaspoons salt, divded
  • 1½ teaspoons of dried basil, crushed
  • ¼ teaspoons of pepper
  • 1 container (15 ounces) of ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon of parsley flakes
  • 4 cups (1 pound) shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ¾ cups of grated parmesan cheese

Steps:

  1. Step 1:

    Cook the pasta according to the directions on the box before draining it.

  2. Step 2:

    Cook italian sausage, onions and garlic in a large skillet over medium-high heat.

  3. Step 3:

    Stir in the tomatoes, tomato paste, sugar, 2 teaspoons of the salt, the basil, fennel seeds(optional) and pepper. Bring to a boil over high heat, before reducing the heat to low and simmer, uncovered, for 20 minutes.

  4. Step 4:

    Blend ricotta, egg, parsley and the remaining ½ teaspoons of salt in a small bowl.

  5. Step 5:

    Spoon 1½ cups of the meat sauce into a 13x9 baking dish. Layer ⅓ each of the lasagna, remaining meat sauce, ricotta mixture, mozzarella and parmesan cheese into the dish. Repeat the layers until you're out of ingredients. Cover the whole thing with foil covered in pam.

  6. Step 6:

    Bake at 375°F for 25 minutes. Peel off the foil and bake it for another 15-20 minutes, or until it's heated through. Let it stand for about ten minutes before serving.
